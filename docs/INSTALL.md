**Concept Topic**

**Purpose**: This guide will help you understand how to use the Telegram bot **"** **Чат-Интерпретатор Лексики | ЧИЛ"** - a tool for automatically detecting and explaining slang expressions in Russian texts. The bot analyzes user messages, finds words from its built-in youth slang dictionary, and provides their definitions. The main function is to recognize slang vocabulary taking into account various grammatical forms of words.

**Target Audience**:

This guide is intended for:

- **Students of linguistics and philology** studying modern Russian language, youth speech, and sociolinguistics.
- **Researchers** working with text corpora and needing a tool for rapid identification of slang vocabulary.
- **Russian language teachers** who want to introduce students to current slang expressions.
- **General users** interested in modern Russian slang and wishing to check their texts for informal vocabulary.

No special technical skills are required. The user only needs a Telegram account and basic knowledge of using the messenger.

**Context of Use**:

**"** **Чат-Интерпретатор Лексики | ЧИЛ"** operates within the Telegram ecosystem and does not require installing additional software. The bot loads the current slang dictionary from an external source each time it starts.

The tool is especially useful in the following situations:

- Analyzing texts for slang expressions
- Educational purposes (introducing youth vocabulary)

This guide covers only the functionality related to searching for slang and obtaining definitions. Other features of the Telegram platform are not considered in this instruction.

**Task Topic**

**Goal**: To learn how to use the Telegram bot **"** **Чат-Интерпретатор Лексики | ЧИЛ"** to search for and explain slang words in text.

**Prerequisites**:

Before you begin, make sure that:

- You have the Telegram messenger installed.
- You have a Telegram account.
- You have access to the Internet.

**Step 1. Find the bot in Telegram**

- Open the Telegram application.
- Click on the search bar (at the top of the screen).
- Enter the bot name: **@ru_slang_bot**
- Select the bot from the search results and click **Start** (or **Начать**).

**Step 2. Familiarize yourself with the available commands**

After launching, the bot will send a welcome message. To get help, use the /help command:

- Type /help in the message input field.
- Send the command by pressing the send button.
- The bot will show detailed instructions on how to use it, including a list of all available commands.

Available bot commands:

- /start - Welcome message and brief instructions
- /help - Detailed help on using the bot
- /stats - Dictionary statistics (word count, source)
- /words - List of all words in the dictionary

_Note_: You can also see the list of all commands in Telegram by typing / in the input field - the messenger will show a hint with the available commands.

**Step 3. Send text for analysis**

- Enter any message containing suspected slang words.  
   _Example:_ "Вчера был такой кринж, просто треш!"
- Send the message by pressing the send button.
- The bot will analyze the text and return a response with a list of found slang words and their definitions.

**Step 4. Analyze the result**

In the bot's response:

- Each found slang word is highlighted in bold.
- Its definition is given on a new line after the word.

_Note_: If no slang words are found in the text, the bot will notify you and suggest trying other words or viewing the entire dictionary with the /words command.

**Additional Information**

**Step 5. View the full dictionary**

To see all the words that the bot recognizes as slang:

- Enter the /words command.
- The bot will output the list of words in parts (50 words per message).
- To get the meaning of a specific word, simply send that word in the chat.

**Step 6. Check dictionary statistics**

To get information about the dictionary:

- Enter the /stats command.
- The bot will show:
  - The total number of words in the dictionary
  - Dictionary language (Russian)
  - The source from which the dictionary was loaded

**Possible Problems and Their Solutions**

**Problem 1: The bot does not respond**

Check your internet connection. The bot may be restarting - try sending the /start command again after 1-2 minutes.

**Problem 2: Slang word not found**

The word may be absent from the current version of the dictionary. Use the /words command to check if the word is present.

**Problem 3: The bot does not find all forms of a word**

The bot supports stemming, but some rare word forms may not be recognized. Try sending the word in its base form.
